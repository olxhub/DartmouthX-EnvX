Course Overview

ENVX is an introductory environmental science course that explores the relationship between people and the natural world. 

Environmental impacts on Earth come from the number of people and the amount and types of resources that they use. By studying human populations, rates of resource consumption and the impact of different types of resources we will gain an understanding of how humans impact Earth. 

ENVX is organized into six sections, originally released one per week.  In this summer offering of the course, Weeks 1-3 will open on June 15th and Weeks 4-6 will open on June 29th.

Each topic begins with an introductory video, followed by a combination of readings, additional videos, quizzes to check your understanding and a section exam. Many of the videos contain examples, diagrams, word slides and animations. There are also numerous opportunities for discussion, some of which are prompted. You accumulate points by completing self-assessments during the section, answering prompted discussion questions and completing the test at the end of the section. 

Weekly Topics

Intro to Environmental Science (Week 1)

In the first section, we will explore the field of environmental science and how to think like an environmental scientist. What is the difference between environmental science and environmental studies? How do both differ from environmentalism? Why is energy so important in environmental science? 

The Human Population (Week 2)

Understanding the human population and the ways in which changes in the population affect the environment is essential to environmental science. How many people are there living on Earth and where do they live? What is the carrying capacity of Earth?  

Food and Soils (Week  3)

Part of understanding human carrying capacity depends on knowledge of agriculture, soils and the environmental implications of eating different types of food. How does eating meat or vegetables, local or organic, sustainable or industrial foods affect the environment?

Non-Renewable Energy (Week 4)

Almost every aspect of our lives is dependent on energy. Non-renewable fossil fuels are an important source of energy in much of the world. What are the advantages and disadvantages to using coal, petroleum and natural gas? How does each contribute to greenhouse gas emissions? 

Renewable Energy (Week 5)

The use of renewable fuels such as wind and solar is growing rapidly around the world.  What are the benefits and consequences  of using each? Are “green” energy sources the answer to the world’s energy needs? 

Global Change (Week 6)

Human impact on biodiversity and global change are arguably the integrating units of environmental science. How are we affecting global change and biodiversity? How can we reconcile human population growth, resource demands and sustainability?
