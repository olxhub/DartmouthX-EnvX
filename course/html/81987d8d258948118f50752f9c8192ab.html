Prompted Discussion

Last week, we learned that energy that "flows through your fingertips" refers to energy that you control directly such as the energy you use when you turn on a light switch, adjust a thermostat, operate a car, use public transportation, or eat food.

This week, we will expand on this idea and ask you to post about a renewable energy source that “flows through your fingertips.” Perhaps your city or country has a large wind farm. Or maybe you own a renewable energy system such as PV panels. 

For example, a search for “New Hampshire electricity fuel mix” returns this website and the following chart: 

New Hampshire obtains approximately 14% of its electricity from renewable sources (6% hydro + 8% biomass). 

Prompted Discussion Assignment

Write a post that presents the renewable energy that "flows through your fingertips.” Include the following information:

    • The country you live in (and if it is a large country, what region).
      • One renewable energy source that “flows through your fingertips.” We recommend you choose electricity and search on the web to find information about the "fuel mix" for your community, region or country (see the New Hampshire example above). 
        • The percentage of your electricity, if any, that comes from renewable sources. Also include the breakdown of renewables by source (hydro, wind, solar and biomass).
          • Or if you know of other renewable sources that “flow through your fingertips,” report on those. For example, you might determine the fraction of your diesel fuel usage that is biodiesel. 
            • Optional: Consider posting a photo of a renewable energy source in your community.

          Finally, ask an environmental science question related to someone else's post about their renewable energy use.
