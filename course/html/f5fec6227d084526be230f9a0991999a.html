Prompted Discussion

Energy that "flows through your fingertips" refers to energy that you control directly such as the energy you use when you turn on a light switch, adjust a thermostat, operate a car, use public transportation, or eat food.

For example, a person who owns an automobile that travels 25 miles using one gallon (U.S.) of gasoline, and who travels 10,000 miles per year*, would report that they use 400 gallons of gasoline per year (see the equation below). A quick search on the web (“convert gasoline to megajoules“) reveals that 400 gallons of gasoline is 52,704 megajoules of energy. If you live in a country that sells gasoline in liters, since there are 3.8 liters per U.S. gallon, you would report that you use 1,520 liters of gasoline per year.
